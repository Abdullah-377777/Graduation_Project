What is Lung Cancer?

Lung cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the lungs, typically in the lining of the air passages. It occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably, forming a tumor that can interfere with breathing and spread to other parts of the body.

In this page, you'll learn more about:

  • Types of Lung Cancer: A breakdown of the main subtypes, including NSCLC and SCLC.
  • Early Warning Signs and Symptoms: Key indicators to watch for early detection.
  • Causes of Lung Cancer: Risk factors that increase the likelihood of developing lung cancer.
  • Prevention Tips: Ways to lower your risk and support long-term lung health.

Recognizing symptoms early and understanding risk factors such as smoking, environmental exposure, and genetic history is essential for prevention and effective treatment.

Overview

The main reason attributed to the development of lung cancer in individuals is smoking which damages the lungs. Upon inhaling cigarette smoke, toxic substances called carcinogens enter the respiratory tract. These alter the genetics, structure and function of healthy lung cells, apart from causing injury to the lining along the lungs.

Worldwide Incidence

According to the World Health Organization, lung cancer is one of the most common cancers globally, accounting for approximately 15% of all cancer cases in men. It is particularly prevalent in developed countries, with higher incidence rates observed in North America and Europe.

Global map indicating the prevalence of lung cancer.

Incidence in Saudi Arabia

In Saudi Arabia, the incidence of lung cancer has been rising steadily, currently accounting for about 12% of all cancer cases reported annually among men. Early detection efforts and awareness campaigns are crucial for improving outcomes in the region.

Types of Lung Cancer

ü´Å Adenocarcinoma

The most common type of lung cancer, especially in non-smokers. It originates in the outer regions of the lungs and usually grows more slowly than other forms.

üß± Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Often associated with smoking, this cancer starts in the flat cells lining the airways and typically develops in the central parts of the lungs.

‚ö° Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)

A fast-growing and aggressive cancer type, strongly linked to smoking. It frequently spreads to other parts of the body early in the disease.

üß¨ Large Cell Carcinoma

A rarer form that can appear in any part of the lung. It tends to grow and spread quickly, often making it more difficult to treat.

Early Warning Signs

These are subtle signs that may appear in the early stages of lung cancer and warrant medical attention:

  • Persistent cough that doesn't go away.
  • Mild shortness of breath during routine activities.
  • Unexplained fatigue or weakness.
  • Mild chest discomfort or pain.

Symptoms in Advanced Stages

As lung cancer progresses, symptoms become more noticeable and may include:

  • Coughing up blood or rust-colored sputum.
  • Severe chest pain.
  • Significant weight loss without trying.
  • Hoarseness or voice changes.
  • Recurrent infections like bronchitis or pneumonia.

Early detection is critical. If you experience any of these signs, consult a healthcare professional promptly.

  • üö¨ Smoking: The leading cause of lung cancer. The risk increases with the number of cigarettes and the length of time a person smokes.
  • üå´Ô∏è Secondhand Smoke: Prolonged exposure to secondhand smoke can increase the risk of lung cancer, even for non-smokers.
  • ‚ò¢Ô∏è Harmful Substances: Exposure to substances like asbestos, radon gas, or other carcinogens at work or home can elevate lung cancer risk.

How to Reduce Risk

Avoid smoking, reduce exposure to harmful chemicals, and consider regular health screenings if you have a family history or environmental risk factors.
